KNEE PAIN

HEAT AND COLD THERAPY

Use a heating pad to relieve knee pain while resting your knee and then use cold treatment to reduce inflammation.The following are some of the tips for applying heat and cold therapy:

1. First use heat pad for up to 15 to 20 minutes.

2. Check that a heat pad isn’t too hot before applying.

3. Heat therapy is not advisable if your joint is warm during a flare.

4. To help ease stiffness in the joint take a warm shower early in the morning.

5. Alternate between heat and cold.

6. Apply cold packs for up to 15 to 20 minutes five to six times a day for the first two days after injury.

7. During the first 24 hours after injury or joint sprain apply cold pack or gel pack more often during the entire day.

8. Never apply ice directly to the skin.

9. You can also apply heat and cold treatment along with ointments containing capsaicin and paraffin.
